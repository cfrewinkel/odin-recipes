Apple-Cheddar Grilled Cheese Sandwiches with Tomato Soup

Description

Grilled cheese fancied up with apple slices. Tomato soup thats better than the can version.

Ingredients

  • 1 (8 oz) block cheddar cheese
  • 16 fl oz chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 (14.5 oz) cans diced tomatoes
  • 2 Gala apples
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 8 slices whole grain bread
  • 1 medium yellow onion
  1. Wash and dry the fresh produce.
  2. Quarter the apples lengthwise, then cut out the core and stem from each section. Cut 2 of the quarters lengthwise into thin wedges and transfer to a small bowl. Medium dice the other 2 quarters and transfer to a medium bowl.
  3. Trim off and discard the ends of the onion and remove the outer layer; small dice the onion (cut into ¼-inch pieces) and add to the bowl with the apples.
  4. Trim off and discard the root ends of the garlic; peel and mince or press the garlic. Add to the bowl with the apples and onion.
  5. Once the saucepan is hot, add butter and swirl to coat the bottom.
  6. Add the apples, onion, and garlic to the saucepan; season with oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook, stirring frequently, until the apples and onion are softened, 4 to 6 minutes.
  7. Add the diced tomatoes and broth to the saucepan; season with additional salt and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer and cook for 8 to 10 minutes.
  8. Add butter to a nonstick skillet and heat over medium-low heat.
  9. Place the bread slices on a clean, dry work surface. Divide the apple slices and cheese between 4 of the bread slices, then top with the remaining bread slices.
  10. Once melted, spread the butter around the skillet, then season the bottom of the skillet with salt.
  11. Place the sandwiches in the skillet and cook until golden brown and crispy on the first side, 3 to 4 minutes. Then, add more butter and salt, and carefully flip the sandwiches; cook until golden brown on the other side and the cheese has melted, 3 to 4 minutes. Once done, transfer to a cutting board. Cook in batches if necessary.
  12. Using a hand blender, purée the soup until smooth. Alternatively, use a potato masher to mash the tomatoes and apples (the soup will be chunkier).
  13. Cut the grilled cheese sandwiches in half diagonally.
  14. To serve, divide the soup between small bowls and serve with the grilled cheese on the side. Enjoy!
